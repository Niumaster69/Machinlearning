Unsupervised Learning — Manual Simulation

K-Means Step by Step
with 100 Video Games

A complete by-hand simulation of K-Means: initial centroids, Euclidean distances, cluster assignments, centroid updates and variance reduction across three iterations.

Dataset

Sample of 100 Video Games

For this manual simulation we take a random sample of 100 games from the Video Games Sales dataset. Each record is described by two numerical features — exactly as required by the activity:

  • Critic_Score — Metacritic critical rating on a 0–100 scale
  • Global_Sales — Worldwide sales in millions of units

The goal is to discover K = 3 natural groups of games based on critical success and commercial success, without using any external label.

Interpretation of the Final Clustering

Cluster 1 — Low critic / low sales: games with weak critical reception and modest commercial performance. These are typically mediocre titles that neither impressed reviewers nor found a wide audience.

Cluster 2 — Mid critic / mid sales: the largest group — solid but not exceptional games, with average review scores and moderate commercial success. This is the "mainstream bulk" of the industry.

Cluster 3 — High critic / high sales: the blockbuster cluster. Games here are critically acclaimed and also sold in very large volumes — the sweet spot every studio aims for.

Convergence behavior: WCSS dropped monotonically across the three iterations, confirming the algorithm is heading toward a stable solution. Beyond iteration 3 the centroids move only slightly, which means three iterations already capture most of the clustering structure.
